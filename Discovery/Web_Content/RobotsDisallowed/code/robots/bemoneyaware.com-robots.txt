        <h2 class="title"><a href="http://www.bemoneyaware.com/blog/4-things-you-need-to-know-before-getting-your-health-insurance/" title="Permalink to 4 things you need to know before getting your health insurance" rel="bookmark">4 things you need to know before getting your health insurance</a></h2>
        
        <div class="entry clearfix">
            
                        
            <p>The marriage between increasing healthcare costs in India and the changing lifestyle of the people has given birth to the dire need of health insurance. However, surprisingly, more than 50% of the population do not have their facts right about this significant investment. Like alligators in the sewers of New York, myths about health insurance just don’t seem to go away.</p>
<p>People believe that only the earning member of the family needs health insurance. They settle for their employer’s group cover and call health insurance as the saviour from only hospitalisation bills. Smokers, consumers of alcohol and the fit and healthy believe health insurance is not for them. None of this is however true. Health insurance is the need of every individual and there is a <a title="Example of Health Insurance" href="https://www.cignattkinsurance.in/" target="_blank">suitable </a><a href="https://www.cignattkinsurance.in/" target="_blank">health insurance policy</a> for everyone.</p>
<p>Here is a list of the critical factors that one must consider before choosing health insurance.</p>
<h3>An honest assessment of your needs</h3>
<p>The first step towards buying health insurance is to decide which members of the family need to be covered and what amount has to be insured. Healthy members with no potential risk of lifestyle or age must still be covered as health insurance protects individuals from any unexpected illness as well as accidents.</p>
<p>For members with high risks, the policy may include certain exclusions or may demand higher premiums. The most common error while choosing the cover is not thinking for the long term. Under normal circumstances your claims will be made several years from now when the inflation has peaked. Therefore it is wise to choose a higher cover to be prepared for the future.</p>

        <div class="entry clearfix">
            
                        
            <p><em>I  have forgotten my UAN Password , my mobile number has changed, What do I do now? On trying to Activate UAN I get message </em><em>Mismatch in UAN and Member ID. What does it mean? My DOB in UAN is wrong. What should I do?</em></p>
<h2>Overview of  UAN</h2>
<ul>
<li>The UAN is a 12-digit number allotted to each Employee Provident Fund member  by the Employee Provident Fund  Organization(EPFO) which gives him control of his EPF account and minimises the role of employer.</li>
<li>Member Id or Member Identification Numbers is the number given by EPFO to allow the employer to submit EPF money of employee. If one changes the job, employer changes, hence Member ID change. Member ID or PF Account Number is in the format given below. PF Account Number <strong>may not</strong> have Extension code. <strong>EPFO Office Code/Establishment Code(Max. 7 Digits)/Extension(Max. 3 digits)/Account Number (Max 7 digit) </strong>Ex:BG/BNG/012345//789.</li>
<li>An employee will have one UAN or Universal Account number, which as the name implies will remain the same. It will maintain all your Member Ids.  Its like you can have multiple Saving Bank account but all these are tied to your one Permanent Account Number or PAN. So when you change your job and the new employer, if contributing to EPF, gives you a new Member ID. This new Member ID has to be linked to your UAN number.</li>
<li>Once you receive the Universal account number or UAN from your employer, you have to log on to the official website <a title="registration of UAN EPFO" href="http://uanmembers.epfoservices.in/" target="_blank">http://uanmembers.epfoservices.in</a>. Click on <em><strong>Activate UAN Based Registration </strong></em>and enter your UAN, mobile number and member ID. Our article <a title="UAN or Universal Account Number and Registration of UAN" href="http://www.bemoneyaware.com/blog/uan-universal-account-number-registration-of-uan/" target="_blank">UAN or Universal Account Number and Registration of UAN</a> explains the process in detail.</li>
<li>Advantages of Registering with UAN are that once you login at the UAN portal,<a title="registration of UAN EPFO" href="http://uanmembers.epfoservices.in/" target="_blank">http://uanmembers.epfoservices.in</a>, you can:
<ul>
<li>You can Download Passbook/UAN Card,</li>
<li>You can List Previous Member ID/View Linking Status,</li>
<li>You can File Transfer Claim/View Transfer Claim Status/System Generated Transfer Claim Status,</li>
<li> You can Edit Mobile Number/Edit Email ID/Update KYC Information/Change Password/Edit Personal Details,</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

<p>Employee Provident Fund  Organization, EPFO has updated  it&#8217;s website <a title="EPFO website" href="http://www.epfindia.gov.in/site_en/" target="_blank">http://www.epfindia.gov.in/site_en/</a>. There is a the <a href="http://www.epfindia.gov.in/site_hi/index.php" target="_blank">Hindi website of EPFO</a> also which one can access. Now EPFO  also include <span style="color: #ff0000;"><em><strong>UAN HelpDesk</strong></em></span>  which is to help one with Common UAN Related problems. However, the UAN HelpDesk is not fully functional but it works.</p>
<ul>
<li>What is my UAN?</li>
<li>Forgot Password and Mobile No is Changed</li>
<li>Incorrect Details &#8211; Name/Father Name/DOB/DOJ etc</li>
<li>Passbook problem</li>
<li>SMS Not received during UAN Activation</li>
<li>Details Not matching During UAN Activation</li>
<li>Claim Status (PF,Transfer,Pension)</li>
<li>Form 11 Pending/UAN linking with Pending with Employer.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Understanding the Travel Insurance Policy</h2>
<p>But, travelling does have its own risks and probabilities of unforeseen situation distress.Often in this excitement of trip we tend to overlook the possibility of getting injured or falling sick. Eventuality can happen anywhere, anytime. So, preparation is a must. <a title="Insurance" href="http://www.bemoneyaware.com/blog/what-one-should-know-about-term-insurance-plans/" target="_blank">Insurance</a> that keeps you covered from any medical expenses, loss of baggage, trip cancellation, flight accident and other losses incurred during your travel within or outside the boundaries of your country is travel insurance. Especially, when you are travelling to a foreign country, you will need to obtain insurance that is applicable to the region with the understanding that things are different everywhere you go. That might be a bit of an obvious statement, but you might not understand just HOW different it can be until you are right in the middle of a bad situation. If you have never purchased this type of insurance before, you obviously would have a lot of questions, and we have brought about five of them for you.</p>

        <h2 class="title"><a href="http://www.bemoneyaware.com/blog/redemption-in-jp-morgan-funds-debt-funds-and-credit-ratings/" title="Permalink to Redemption in JP Morgan Funds, Debt Funds and Credit Ratings" rel="bookmark">Redemption in JP Morgan Funds, Debt Funds and Credit Ratings</a></h2>
        
        <div class="entry clearfix">
            
                        
            <p>One can redeem in Mutual Funds units any time. You might have to pay exit Load but no one can stop you from redeeming those units. But on Aug 28 2015,  JP Morgan Mutual Fund restricted redemptions from two of its debt schemes – Short Term Income Fund and India Treasury Fund. Why did JP Morgan do that? Is this an isolated case ? Are Debt funds risk free? What is a credit rating?</p>
<p><strong>Why did JP Morgan restrict the redemptions in its Debt Funds?</strong></p>
<p>The redemptions in the two schemes of JP Morgan,Short Term Income Fund and India Treasury Fund, was limited (&#8220;gated&#8221;) to a percentage limit not exceeding 1% of the total number of units outstanding on any Business Day.  Any units which consequently are not redeemed on a particular Business Day will, subject to the further application of the Trustee Company&#8217;s right to limit Redemption, be carried forward for Redemption to the next Business Day.As of June 2015, JP Morgan MF has average assets under management of ₹14,684 crore.</p>
<p>The move followed a decline in NAVs (net assets value) of the schemes due to fund house’s exposure to troubled auto component firm Amtek Auto’s,an automobile and ancillary company, debt papers.The decline in NAVs was triggered after a rating agency withdrew its rating on these debt instruments from  A+ to C. Amtek Auto in June quarter results reported a loss of more than Rs.150 Cr. The company is finding it difficult to pay the interest on loans of around Rs.7, 800 Cr. Per quarter they need around Rs.200 Cr. to pay as an interest towards this loan, i.e. around 25% of its sales.</p>
<p>Both the JP Morgan schemes are managed by the fund manager Ravi Ratanpal. The India Short Term Income Fund (launched in March, 2010), in particular, has 15.37% (or Rs 134 crore) exposures to debt papers issued by Amtek Auto with coupon of 10.25%. JP Morgan&#8217;s India Treasury Fund (which completes its seven years next month), on the other hand, a relatively bigger scheme with AUM of Rs 2,534 crore as on 31 July, 2015 has 5.29% (or Rs 66 crore) exposure to 10.25% Amtek Auto 2015 debentures.Both these schemes, put together, have an exposure of Rs 200 crore or about 6.75% of total assets under management (AUM) in debt issuances by financially stressed Amtek Auto. The shares of the company lost 70% on the stock exchanges in Aug 3015. For a scheme of a size of mere Rs 430 crore, such a large exposure to a single security is more than enough to dramatically impact the performance of the scheme if the underlying asset class faces troubles.</p>
<p>While the fund house had allowed redemptions from the two troubled schemes till August 27 and three large corporate were allowed to exit till then, the restriction was imposed on Friday (August 28) and several large corporates including ITC, promoters of HCL Tech and Hero Motocorp were not allowed to exit on Friday. The source also confirmed that JP Morgan had informed the regulator about the imposition of restriction on redemption in the evening on August 28.</p>
<p>It is one of the rarest developments in recent years in the fund industry. The capital markets regulator, Securities and Exchange Board of India (Sebi) has initiated its investigation into the matter.</p>

        <h2 class="title"><a href="http://www.bemoneyaware.com/blog/epf-withdrawal-before-5-years-tds-form-15g-tax-itr/" title="Permalink to EPF Withdrawal before 5 years,TDS,Form 15G,Tax and ITR" rel="bookmark">EPF Withdrawal before 5 years,TDS,Form 15G,Tax and ITR</a></h2>
        
        <div class="entry clearfix">
            
                        
            <p>An individual has to pay tax on withdrawal of PF accumulations if the same has been withdrawn from a recognized PF account without rendering continuous services for five years or more with the employer. We ar e asked two questions most frequently, I am leaving job after so many years can I claim my EPF,  Is EPF balance taxable, Why is my employer is asking to fill Form 15, what is Form 15G, Can I claim TDS? How to claim TDS,How to show PF withdrawal while filing income tax return.</p>
<p><strong>When can one withdraw from EPF?</strong></p>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;"><b>The rules are that an employee should not be in employment for two months after resigning if he has to withdraw his Provident Fund amount. One can withdraw only after a waiting period of two months after resigning. </b>Earlier one could withdraw EPF from one job even after joining another job as EPFO could not track if one is working or had a PF account. But now with UAN number, EPFO can find out whether you are employed or not and hence EPFO can theoretically reject your application. Remember that EPF is a long term retirement investment product, and transfer of account will help you get the magic of compounding.Our article <a href="http://www.bemoneyaware.com/blog/uan-universal-account-number-registration-of-uan/" rel="bookmark">UAN or Universal Account Number and Registration of UAN</a>  explains the UAN number in detail.</p>
<p><strong>Is two months waiting period applicable in all cases?</strong></p>
<p>No. In following cases 2 month waiting period for applying for EPF withdrawal is waived off.</p>
<ul>
<li>Those employees who are going abroad for employment/settlement and they don’t intend to return soon, can Apply for immediate PF withdrawal.</li>
<li>Woman can Withdraw money if she is leaving service for marriage . You have to provide proof of the marriage such as wedding card.</li>
<li>These are after the retirement or death of the employee.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>How are the 5 years calculated?</strong></p>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;">5 years mean 5 years of contribution to EPF. Say you worked in an organization for 3 years and then left it for higher studies. Then you have contributed to EPF for 3 years old. <strong>Just holding the PF account with the employer for five years will not make it 5 years and hence shall not take away the tax implications. But  your EPF would earn interest for 3 years after your last contribution.</strong> So If you intend to take job after some time(say after higher studies) you can leave your PF account and after joining transfer it to new employer.</p>
<p><strong>What happens if I don&#8217;t withdraw from the EPF account after leaving the job? </strong></p>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;"><strong>When you leave the job, you stop contributing to EPF account and your EPF account becomes inactive.</strong> But your EPF would earn interest for 3 years after your last contribution. <span style="color: #ff0000;"><strong>After 3 years your EPF account becomes dormant. Many frauds have been reported of siphoning off the money from the dormant EPF accounts</strong></span></p>
