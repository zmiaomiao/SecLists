<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://blog.friendfeed.com/2009/08/custom-friendfeed-themes-and-way-to.html'>Custom FriendFeed themes and a way to show them off</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header-line-1'><span class='post-author vcard'>
Posted by
<span class='fn'>Casey</span>
</span>
<span class='post-timestamp'>
on
<a class='timestamp-link' href='http://blog.friendfeed.com/2009/08/custom-friendfeed-themes-and-way-to.html' rel='bookmark' title='permanent link'><abbr class='published' title='2009-08-13T15:41:00-07:00'>Thursday, August 13, 2009</abbr></a>
</span>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<p><p>When we <a href="http://blog.friendfeed.com/2009/06/friendfeed-now-6x-more-awesomer-with.html">launched FriendFeed themes</a> we mentioned some improvements we wanted to make, and here they are!</p>

<p><b>Choose how people see your FriendFeed profile</b></p>

<p>Starting today, people who visit your profile will see it in the theme you've chosen. If you'd prefer visitors not to see your theme, you can turn it off on your <a href="http://friendfeed.com/?settings=1">settings page</a>. You can also choose themes for the groups that you're an admin for (looks like the <a href="http://friendfeed.com/so-you-think-you-can-dance">So You Think You Can Dance</a> admin wasted no time!).</p>

<div style="margin:1em;text-align:center"><img src="http://friendfeed-media.com/611f280b5ca07d776b8f1e9de630afc67c0da890" style="width:485px;height:252px;border:0"/></div>

<p>If you'd rather not see other people's profiles, you can make that selection in your <a href="http://friendfeed.com/?settings=1">settings page</a>.</p>

<p><b>Create your own themes</b></p>

<p>Of course, we know a lot of users were looking for even more ways to express themselves, so we've added an option that lets you create a <a href="http://friendfeed.com/settings/themes">custom theme</a> by uploading a background image and choosing a few colors. We want to add more options in the future, but hopefully, <a href="http://friendfeed.com/parislemon/63a15d43/remember-when-i-made-this-can-t-wait-for">this</a> will give you some ideas for where to get started.</p>

<p>As always, let us know of any problems in the <a href="http://friendfeed.com/friendfeed-feedback">feedback group</a> and show us your stuff in the <a href="http://friendfeed.com/friendfeed-themes">themes group</a>.</p></p>

</span>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<p><p>We are happy to announce that <a href="http://www.facebook.com/">Facebook</a> has acquired FriendFeed. As my mom explained to me, when two companies love each other very much, they form a structured investment vehicle...</p>
<div style="margin:1em;text-align:center"><img src="http://friendfeed-media.com/0e65e849ffa91891c42628e7d586476dc7048b25" style="width:290px;height:112px;border:0"/></div>
<p>The FriendFeed team is extremely excited to become a part of the talented Facebook team. We've always been great admirers of Facebook, and our companies share a common vision. Now we have the opportunity to bring many of the innovations we've developed at FriendFeed to Facebook's 250 million users around the world and to work alongside Facebook's passionate engineers to create even more ways for you to easily share with your friends online.</p>
<p><b>What does this mean for my FriendFeed account?</b></p>
<p><a href="http://friendfeed.com/">FriendFeed.com</a> will continue to operate normally. We're still figuring out our longer-term plans for the product with the Facebook team. As usual, we will communicate openly about our plans as they develop &mdash; keep an eye on the <a href="http://friendfeed.com/friendfeed-news">FriendFeed News group</a> for updates.</p>
<p><b>What about the FriendFeed API?</b></p>
<p>The <a href="http://friendfeed.com/api/">FriendFeed API</a> will also continue to operate normally. As above, we will let you know as we settle on our plan to more fully integrate with Facebook.</p>
<p>Check out the <a href="http://www.facebook.com/press/releases.php?p=116581">official press release</a> for more information.</p></p>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://blog.friendfeed.com/2009/08/friendfeed-api-v2-out-of-beta-oauth-for.html'>FriendFeed API v2: Out of beta, OAuth for installed applications</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header-line-1'><span class='post-author vcard'>
Posted by
<span class='fn'>Benjamin</span>
</span>
<span class='post-timestamp'>
on
<a class='timestamp-link' href='http://blog.friendfeed.com/2009/08/friendfeed-api-v2-out-of-beta-oauth-for.html' rel='bookmark' title='permanent link'><abbr class='published' title='2009-08-05T09:51:00-07:00'>Wednesday, August 05, 2009</abbr></a>
</span>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<p><p>Two weeks ago, <a title="we launched" href="http://blog.friendfeed.com/2009/07/friendfeed-api-v2-real-time-oauth-file.html" id="phf6">we launched</a> version 2 of the <a href="http://friendfeed.com/api/">FriendFeed API</a> in beta. Since then, we've watched how developers have been using the API and collected a lot of <a title="their feedback" href="http://friendfeed.com/friendfeed-api-v2" id="dgc9">their feedback</a>. We've implemented some changes, and now, we're ready to remove the beta label!</p>

<p>In addition to removing the beta label, we also added support for using OAuth in applications installed on a user's desktop or device. The way it works is similar to Google's <a href="http://code.google.com/apis/accounts/docs/AuthForInstalledApps.html">ClientLogin for Installed Applications</a>; read all about it in our <a href="http://friendfeed.com/api/documentation#installed_app_auth">API documentation</a>.</p>

<p>During the beta period, a number of developers took advantage of the new API to develop nifty applications. Some of the ones we found most interesting include:</p>

<ul><li><a href="http://dev.ctor.org/f2p/">f2p</a>: A FriendFeed mobile interface built by <a href="http://friendfeed.com/nahi">Hiroshi Nakamura</a>.</li><li><a href="http://www.friendboo.com/">FriendBoo</a>: Post audio updates to your FriendFeed from your phone. Created by <a href="http://friendfeed.com/kinlan">Paul Kinlan</a>.</li><li><a href="http://yungsang.com/friendfoo/v2/">FriendFoo</a>: A real-time FriendFeed interface by <a href="http://friendfeed.com/yungsang">YungSang</a>.</li><li><a href="http://code.google.com/p/friendfeed-api-v2-php/">PHP</a> and <a href="http://code.google.com/p/friendfeed-java-api/">Java</a> libraries built by <a href="http://friendfeed.com/kordi">Nikolai Kordulla</a> and Sean Sullivan respectively.
</li></ul>

<p>And more are on the way! If you have feedback or thoughts on our API, let us know in the <a href="http://friendfeed.com/friendfeed-api">FriendFeed API group</a> and be sure to join the <a href="http://groups.google.com/group/friendfeed-api">developer mailing list</a>.</p></p>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://blog.friendfeed.com/2009/07/improve-friendfeed-for-your-friends-by.html'>Improve FriendFeed for your friends by recommending subscriptions</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header-line-1'><span class='post-author vcard'>
Posted by
<span class='fn'>Bret Taylor</span>
</span>
<span class='post-timestamp'>
on
<a class='timestamp-link' href='http://blog.friendfeed.com/2009/07/improve-friendfeed-for-your-friends-by.html' rel='bookmark' title='permanent link'><abbr class='published' title='2009-07-24T12:36:00-07:00'>Friday, July 24, 2009</abbr></a>
</span>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<p><p>As a devoted FriendFeed user, I have tried to convince all of my friends and family to join the site, but a handful of them never quite got their accounts set up properly. With our new <a href="http://friendfeed.com/friends/recommend">Recommend friends</a> feature, I can fix their FriendFeed experience by recommending subscriptions to them.</p>
<p>Visit <a href="http://friendfeed.com/friends/recommend">http://friendfeed.com/friends/recommend</a>, and you can recommend subscriptions to any of your friends. I found out my dad wasn't subscribed to a number of our family members, so I sent him ten recommendations:</p>
<div style="margin:1em;text-align:center"><a href="http://friendfeed.com/friends/recommend"><img src="http://friendfeed-media.com/6fc0ba677a911f4e1cdbe233157296bbccfbd0ff" style="border:0" alt="Screenshot"/></a></div>
<p>When you send friend recommendations, your friend will get an email with all of your recommendations, including a link to subscribe to all your recommendations with a single click. (This is the best part for me &mdash; it lets me do all the work for my dad, and he just needs to click a single link to get his account in good shape).</p>
<p>You will also find "Recommend friends" links in the popup bubbles that appear when you hover over someone's name:</p>
<div style="margin:1em;text-align:center"><a href="http://friendfeed.com/friends/recommend"><img src="http://friendfeed-media.com/0b9054be966ae55248efd712086ff785ebdab57e" style="border:0" alt="Screenshot"/></a></div>
<p>And you will see them on profile pages of your friends who joined the site recently:</p>
<div style="margin:1em;text-align:center"><a href="http://friendfeed.com/friends/recommend"><img src="http://friendfeed-media.com/4bd6592d9223e5056bff1110339b8bfd07480c27" style="border:0" alt="Screenshot"/></a></div>
<p>You can currently only recommend friends to people who are also subscribed back to you. Let us know what you think in the <a href="http://friendfeed.com/friendfeed-feedback">FriendFeed Feedback group</a>.</p></p>
