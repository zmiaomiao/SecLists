                    <div class="vheading">Search Tips</div>
                    <p align="justify">1.         Jump directly to a  chapter or verse just by entering it into the box. Try any abbreviation, down to even a single letter... for example try <strong>j3</strong> or <strong>h11</strong> or <strong>R7</strong> <strong>niv</strong> or <strong>mt9</strong> <strong>KJV</strong>. </p>
                    <p align="justify">2.         Combine key words, books and chapters as desired ... for example try <strong>Acts 27 ship</strong>.</p>
                    <p align="justify">3.           Use quotation marks to search a specific phrase. <strong>&quot;In the beginning God&quot;</strong> returns only exact matches in that word order.</p>
                    <p align="justify">4.           Use a minus sign to skip entries with a particular word. Try <strong>Paul an apostle -epistles</strong></p>
                    <p align="justify">5.           Use <strong>NT</strong> or <strong>OT</strong> for a particular testament.</p>
                    <p align="justify">6.           Combine books and or chapters with key words. Example: <strong>eph 3 faith</strong>.</p>
                    <p align="justify">7. Narrow your search by including any of these sections: <strong>Law</strong>, <strong>History</strong>, <strong>Prophets</strong>, <strong>Poetry</strong>, <strong>Gospels</strong>, <strong>Acts</strong>, <strong>Epistles</strong>, <strong>Revelation</strong>.</p>
                    <p align="justify">8. Once  you make a search you will be provided more options such as <strong>Strong's</strong>, <strong>Topical</strong>, <strong>OT</strong> and <strong>NT</strong>.</p>
