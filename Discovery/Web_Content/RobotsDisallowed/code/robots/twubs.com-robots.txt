               <h1 class="first"><a href="/p/what-is-a-hashtag" title="What is a hashtag">What is a hashtag</a>?</h1>
               <h2>And how do I use them?</h2>
            </div>
        </div>
    </div>

    <div class="wrap">
       <div class="card">
           <div class="card-content clearfix">
            
                <section class="first">
                    <h2 class="wrap-h2 first">So what are hashtags?</h2>
                    <div class="left">
                        <p>
                            Hashtags are any word in the social conversation that begins with the "#" symbol.   Americans refer to it as a "pound sign", but it's known as a "hash" pretty much everywhere else.  A "#" in front of a word like "politics" makes it a hashtag: <a href="/politics">#politics</a>!  So what are they used for?
                        <p>
                        <p>
                            Hashtags are everywhere.  Politicians have them (<a href="/Obama">#Obama</a>), sport leagues (<a href="/Superbowl">#Superbowl</a>, <a href="/NBA">#NBA</a>) and television shows like "American Idol" (<a href="/idol">#idol</a>).  Hashtags simply let you associate your tweets with a larger topic, group or conversation. It's like adding keywords to your message to make it globally searchable.
                        </p>
                    </div>
                    <div class="right">
                         <img src="//s3.amazonaws.com/static.twubs.com/types/PublicWhatAreHashtags/comiccon.png" alt="What is a Hashtag Tools" />
                    </div>
                </section>

                <section class="gray">
                    <h2 class="wrap-h2">When should I use a hashtag?</h2>
                    <div class="left">
                        <p>
                            You should use a hashtag when you are trying to talk about a specific topic.  You can use one that's floating around and join in on that conversation, or completely make up your own and get a new topic started.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                            If you are trying to make a specific point about any of the hashtags to the right, it’s a good idea to use the hashtag.  Doing so will enable others to find your contribution to the global conversation without having to know or follow you.
                        </p>
                    </div>
                    <div class="right">
                        <p>
                            Hashtags usually refer to things like:</p>
                        <ul>
                            <li>
                                Subjects:
                                <a href="/freedom">#freedom</a>,
                                <a href="/biotech">#biotech</a>,
                                <a href="/news">#news</a>,
                                etc.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                                Events:
                                <a href="/SXSW">#SXSW</a>,
                                <a href="/Olympics">#Olympics</a>,
                                <a href="/Election">#Election</a>,
                                etc.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                                Places:
                                <a href="/NYC">#NYC</a>,
                                <a href="/LAX">#LAX</a>,
                                <a href="/London">#London</a>,
                                etc.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                                Things:
                                <a href="/coffee">#coffee</a>,
                                <a href="/computers">#computers</a>,
                                <a href="/cars">#cars</a>,
                                etc.
                            </li>
                            <li>
                                Verbs:
                                <a href="/golfing">#golfing</a>,
                                <a href="/cooking">#cooking</a>,
                                <a href="/flying">#flying</a>,
                                etc.
                            </li>
                        </ul>
                    </div>
                </section>

                <section>
                    <h2 class="wrap-h2">How do I use hashtags?</h2>
                    <div class="left">
                        <p>
                            Using hashtags is easy.  You simply put the "#" sign in front of whatever it is you are trying to associate your tweet with.  
                        </p>
                         <p>
                            <strong>International Friends:</strong> We do our best to support all standard international characters, but we are not perfect. If you
                            run into a situation where we are not recognizing a valid hashtag due to international characters, please <a href="/p/contact">let us know</a>.
                        </p>
                    </div>
                    <div class="right">
                        <p>
                            Here are some general rules to making a hashtag:
                        </p>
                        <ol>
                            <li>No spaces or punctuation characters.
                            <li>Underscores are OK
                            <li>Standard international characters are OK
                            <li>No crazy characters like (╯°□°）╯︵ ┻━┻
                        </ol>
                    </div>
                </section>
