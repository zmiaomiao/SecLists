								<h4>Step-by-Step: How to save your data using Google Takeout</h4>
								<p>You can use Google Takeout to save files and data associated with your wildblue.net or exede.net email account. Google Takeout will save your documents, presentations, photos, contacts, and other Google Apps to a folder on your computer.</p>
								<p><span class="label label-primary">Please Note</span> This is for customers who are using @wildblue.net or @exede.net email accounts only. If you are using a regular Gmail account with an email address ending in @gmail.com, there are no changes being made to your email account or any of the services connected to it.</p>
								<p>To help you save your data, we have created step-by-step instructions on how to export and save your data using Google Takeout.</p>
								<ol>
									<li>Visit the <a href="https://www.google.com/settings/takeout" target="_blank">Google Takeout</a> website and login using your wildblue.net or exede.net account with your existing username and password.</li>
									<li>Once logged in, you will be directed to a page that has a list of all of the Google Apps that were available to you through the ISP edition (exede.net or wildblue.net) of Google Apps. </li>
									<li>Make sure there is a checkmark next to each Google App with data that you wish to export. </li>
									<li>When you are brought to the next screen, click the &ldquo;Create Archive&rdquo; button. Google Takeout will then take a few minutes to compress your files into a folder. </li>
									<li>After the folder is created, you will be able to click &ldquo;Download&rdquo; to save it to your computer. An email with the link to your data will also be sent to your wildblue.net or exede.net account. (This may take some time, up to 24 hours.)</li>
									<li>You will be asked if you want to save or open the file. Select &ldquo;Save&rdquo; and store the file where you can easily find it. It&rsquo;s important to download and save the file to complete the process. Note: This may use a significant amount of data depending on the size of the file(s). If your Exede plan has a Free Zone, you may wish to do this download during those hours.</li>
									<li>Once you&rsquo;ve saved the file, you can go into the saved folder and browse your Google Apps data. You can choose to use the data with another program (such as Microsoft Office), a new or existing Gmail account (@gmail.com), or hold onto it for your records. </li>
								</ol>
								<p>For more information on downloading your data visit <a href="https://support.google.com/accounts/answer/3024190" target="_blank">Google Support</a>.</p>

									<a role="button" data-toggle="collapse" data-parent="#accordion" href="#collapsePop" aria-expanded="true" aria-controls="collapsePop">
										The difference between POP3 and IMAP email settings
									</a>
								</h4>
							</div>
							<div id="collapsePop" class="panel-collapse collapse" role="tabpanel" aria-labelledby="headingPop">
								<div class="panel-body">
									<p><span class="label label-primary">Summary</span> This article can help you decide whether you want to use POP3 or IMAP settings with your email client.</p>
									<p>If you have ever gone into your email settings, it is likely you have been asked to make the choice between POP3 and IMAP.  What do these settings really mean?</p>
									<ul class="fa-ul">
										<li>
											<i class="fa-li fa fa-angle-double-right text-orange"></i>
											<p>The &ldquo;POP&rdquo; in <strong>POP3 </strong>stands for Post Office Protocol and this setting works just like the Post Office of the digital world. When a POP3 server delivers a message; it only delivers it once, and it can only be accessed from that location. POP3 deletes emails from the server as soon as they are downloaded.</p>
											<p>This could cause problems if you want to open an email on your phone that you previously viewed on your laptop. If you are someone who wants to have access to all of your emails though all of your devices (phone, laptop, tablet, computer, etc.) you should consider using IMAP settings.</p>
										</li>
										<li>
											<i class="fa-li fa fa-angle-double-right text-orange"></i>
											<p><strong>IMAP</strong>, which stands for Internet Message Access Protocol, can be useful for people with multiple email clients and webmail interfaces. With IMAP, your messages are kept on a remote server and every time you check your inbox, all of your emails are downloaded from the server to your device. IMAP syncs all of your emails so that your activity (reading, moving and deleting) is the same across all locations.</p>
										</li>
									</ul>
									<p>POP3 may be better for those with smaller inboxes or limited data on their internet service plans, because each email only downloads once. IMAP downloads your email in each location, which uses up a little more data each time you check your email. Issues sending and receiving email can also occur if your inbox is full.</p>
									<p>In short, POP3 stores your mail locally on whichever device you used to check it, while IMAP stores everything remotely on a server than can be accessed through any of your devices.</p>
