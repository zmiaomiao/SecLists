			<div class="entry-meta">
									<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="https://blogmeinblog.wordpress.com/2013/08/02/introducing-self-healing-alerts-in-logmein-central/" title="9:58 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">August 2, 2013</span></a>					<span class="by-author"><span class="sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="https://blogmeinblog.wordpress.com/author/caragoodwin/" title="View all posts by Cara Goodwin" rel="author">Cara Goodwin</a></span> </span>							</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

				<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Eliminate the painstaking process of fixing individual issues manually as they arise with new Self-Healing Alerts in LogMeIn Central.</p>
<p>By combining two of Central’s most powerful features: One2Many, which allows you to automate routine tasks across multiple computers simultaneously and Alerts, which allows you to proactively monitor the health of the computers you manage, Self-Healing Alerts does the work so you don’t have to.</p>
<p>Simply create the alert for the situation you want to monitor, write the script to solve the issue and then combine the two. Now, whenever your most time-consuming, routine but necessary maintenance tasks arise the problem is fixed before you know about it.</p>
<p>Check out the Self-Healing Alerts video and let us know what you think.</p>

			<div id="post-1887" class="post-1887 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-elevate-channel-program category-vision tag-channel tag-channel-program tag-elevate tag-logmein tag-logmein-elevate tag-msp">
							<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="https://blogmeinblog.wordpress.com/2013/07/23/the-new-logmein-channel-program-a-behind-the-scenes-look/" rel="bookmark">The New LogMeIn Channel Program:  A Behind the Scenes&nbsp;Look</a></h2>			
			<div class="entry-meta">
									<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="https://blogmeinblog.wordpress.com/2013/07/23/the-new-logmein-channel-program-a-behind-the-scenes-look/" title="4:04 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">July 23, 2013</span></a>					<span class="by-author"><span class="sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="https://blogmeinblog.wordpress.com/author/shannonmayer2013/" title="View all posts by Shannon Kohn Mayer" rel="author">Shannon Kohn Mayer</a></span> </span>							</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

				<div class="entry-content">
				<p><a href="https://blogmeinblog.files.wordpress.com/2013/07/elevate.jpg"><span class='embed-youtube' style='text-align:center; display: block;'><iframe class='youtube-player' type='text/html' width='640' height='390' src='https://www.youtube.com/embed/EOXWn9DLeNM?version=3&#038;rel=1&#038;fs=1&#038;showsearch=0&#038;showinfo=1&#038;iv_load_policy=1&#038;wmode=transparent' frameborder='0' allowfullscreen='true'></iframe></span></a></p>
<p><a style="color:#ff4b33;font-size:16px;line-height:24px;" href="https://blogmeinblog.files.wordpress.com/2013/07/elevate.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-1889" alt="logmein elevate channel program" src="https://blogmeinblog.files.wordpress.com/2013/07/elevate.jpg?w=300&#038;h=127" width="300" height="127" /></a></p>
<p>In case you missed it, LogMeIn has officially announced a new channel program to help MSPs, VARs, and other outsourced IT service providers better capitalize on the growing shift to cloud-based apps and employee-introduced technology.  The program might be new, but the people behind – and LogMeIn itself – have enjoyed relationships with IT service providers that in some cases, go back decades.  It was this experience and these relationships that attracted many of us, including myself, to LogMeIn in the first place.   So what exactly is the channel program and what’s in it for MSPs, VARs, and IT service providers?</p>
<p>I sat down with Ted Roller, LogMeIn’s VP of channel development, and a former MSP himself, to discuss why this new program was created and how it will help service providers bring on new services for today’s cloud-centric workplace.</p>
<p><b>Mayer: Why introduce a channel program into the market now?</b></p>
<p><b>Roller: </b><i>Cloud applications have changed the way technology is introduced into the workplace and the way workers look to address their needs around collaboration, productivity and communication.  Nearly 70% of SMBs report the use of employee-introduced apps actively being used at work – something known as the bring-your-own-app (BYOA) trend. </i></p>
<p><i>As the new wave of BYOD and BYOA continues to emerge, IT service providers must realign their management mindset – and related offerings – from simply managing devices on a defined network to managing users. They need to evolve their business to focus on the deployment, management and integration of cloud apps.</i></p>
<p><i>With this in mind, the LogMeIn Elevate channel program is dedicated to helping businesses evolve to deliver user-centric IT services in the cloud, empower BYOA and BYOD across their client’s business, and meet the demands of the cloud environment.</i></p>
<p><b>M: How will LogMeIn Elevate help MSPs, VARs and other IT service providers do this?</b></p>
<p><b>R: </b><i>Our goals are simple:</i></p>
<ol>
<li><i></i><i><span style="text-decoration:underline;">Help IT service providers make the shift to user-centric management</span>. We believe the only way to do this effectively is to center IT management on the user, thus ensuring that no matter where they access information or how they share it, IT is in position to enable, secure, and support use on the terms of the business. </i></li>
<li><i style="color:#444444;line-height:1.5;"><span style="text-decoration:underline;">Redefine RMM and how it’s delivered.</span> The monitoring of networks and the devices on them has been a proven profit center for MSPs, but the changing dynamics of today’s workplace threatens to diminish the value of classic RMM. LogMeIn’s Elevate program is committed to helping IT service providers augment RMM to meet the needs of the modern cloud-centric workplace through remote management of cloud apps, devices and users.</i></li>
<li><i style="color:#444444;line-height:1.5;"><span style="text-decoration:underline;">Give IT service providers the tools and education needed to deliver productivity benefits, expand their offerings, and create new revenue streams within their customer case.</span> By delivering value-added management benefits atop the dozens of cloud apps at use in today’s workplace, and becoming a trusted source for new productivity benefits, Elevate will put our channel partners in position to increase revenue, margins and influence in the cloud app era.</i></li>
</ol>
<p><b>M:  Upon sign up, what can channel partners expect to receive? </b></p>
<p><b>R:</b> <i>We’re rolling this out in phases, but the goal is to help our channel partners create and effectively market profitable services around user support, user management and user productivity.  To help with user support, we’re offering special monthly pricing on LogMeIn’s remote support solution, LogMeIn Rescue + Mobile, giving them options to deliver higher margin services to support users and cloud app usage across personal or work PCs, Macs, smartphones and tablets. Partners will also have early access to AppGuru, LogMeIn’s cloud app management solution, giving them the ability to manage users and policies across cloud apps, no matter where they are accessed or used. In addition they will receive marketing support to help create and sell new managed services, access to LogMeIn’s product and development organizations to influence future channel product offerings, dedicated tech support to quickly resolve issues, and lastly, channel-specific education to accelerate ROI. </i></p>
<p><i>A second phase beginning later in 2013  will introduce a reseller program for LogMeIn’s popular collaboration and productivity app, as well as 3rd party cloud app management capabilities through a channel-focused version of LogMeIn’s new IT offering, AppGuru. </i></p>
<p>Interested in chatting with us about the LogMeIn Elevate program? We’ll be on the road at <a href="http://www.comptia.org/channelcon">CompTIA ChannelCon</a>, July 29-31 – stop by our booth if you’re attending!  To learn more information on the program, click <a href="https://secure.logmein.com/welcome/about/channel/">here.</a><i></i></p>

			<div id="post-1879" class="post-1879 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-app-management category-appguru category-it-management tag-cloud tag-cloud-security tag-collaboration tag-it tag-it-management-2">
							<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="https://blogmeinblog.wordpress.com/2013/07/19/your-dropbox-problem-is-bigger-than-dropbox/" rel="bookmark">Your Dropbox Problem is Bigger than&nbsp;Dropbox</a></h2>			
			<div class="entry-meta">
									<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="https://blogmeinblog.wordpress.com/2013/07/19/your-dropbox-problem-is-bigger-than-dropbox/" title="4:25 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">July 19, 2013</span></a>					<span class="by-author"><span class="sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="https://blogmeinblog.wordpress.com/author/ebisceglia/" title="View all posts by Eric Bisceglia" rel="author">Eric Bisceglia</a></span> </span>							</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

				<div class="entry-content">
				<p><span style="line-height:1.5;"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-1883" alt="fight the fud" src="https://blogmeinblog.files.wordpress.com/2013/07/fight-the-fud.gif?w=232&#038;h=300" width="232" height="300" />Yet another </span><a style="line-height:1.5;" href="http://www.nbcnews.com/technology/dropbox-used-chinese-hackers-spread-malware-6C10642402">cloud security scare story</a><span style="line-height:1.5;"> has been making the rounds. In this particular case, Chinese hackers targeted users by sharing a malicious file from free Dropbox accounts. While it </span>wasn&#8217;t<span style="line-height:1.5;"> your traditional hack – Dropbox was not compromised and its users’ and customers’ data was never at risk &#8212; it was enough to trigger the usual slew of sensationalized stories in the press and it was enough FUD to make business leaders nervous.</span></p>
<p>Now that the dust has settled, how many IT professionals had to answer the question, “we use that app, so what are you doing to make sure we’re safe”? If you didn&#8217;t get asked this time, I’m sure it has happened before. If it hasn&#8217;t, it will. It’s an unfortunate inevitability.</p>
<p>The truth of the matter is that the problem here is bigger than Dropbox. Employees are using a number of cloud apps to collaborate, share sensitive information, and get stuff done, from more ‘consumerized’ apps like Dropbox and Evernote to super simple business apps like Trello and Basecamp. The characteristics of these employee-introduced apps represent a much larger issue, a much more disruptive trend that is forcing IT professionals to re-imagine what makes a great business app.</p>
<p>I think we can all agree that we&#8217;ve reached a point where employee introduced cloud apps are unavoidable in the workplace. They’re just too darn simple and useful, and the free-to-low-cost price tag certainly doesn&#8217;t hurt. But IT pros need a better way to monitor and understand what apps employees are using. This isn&#8217;t because they should be playing Big Brother or actively looking to block usage, but rather because they need to be better prepared to respond when these issues arise. And for the apps that businesses choose to endorse and use, there’s a need for robust controls that might help to avoid some of the stumbles and mitigate some inherent risks when using such apps.</p>
<p>For you IT pros out there, have you ever had to respond to the backlash such stories tend to create?  If so, how did you make your boss or business leaders feel at ease with your company’s approach/readiness?</p>

			<div class="entry-meta">
									<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="https://blogmeinblog.wordpress.com/2013/06/27/appguru-catalog-demo/" title="11:09 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">June 27, 2013</span></a>					<span class="by-author"><span class="sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="https://blogmeinblog.wordpress.com/author/ebisceglia/" title="View all posts by Eric Bisceglia" rel="author">Eric Bisceglia</a></span> </span>							</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

				<div class="entry-content">
				<p style="text-align:center;"><span class='embed-youtube' style='text-align:center; display: block;'><iframe class='youtube-player' type='text/html' width='640' height='390' src='https://www.youtube.com/embed/i_8kpD9FlzQ?version=3&#038;rel=1&#038;fs=1&#038;showsearch=0&#038;showinfo=1&#038;iv_load_policy=1&#038;wmode=transparent' frameborder='0' allowfullscreen='true'></iframe></span></p>
<p>AppGuru is designed to give IT a single, central source for managing the myriad cloud apps being adopted and used in the workplace. And as I noted in my last <a href="http://b.logme.in/2013/06/20/new-innovation-in-the-appguru-preview-part-1-group-support/" target="_blank">post</a>, we’re actively listening to our users to help guide our decisions on functionality and AppGuru’s ever-expanding app catalog.  So, with that in mind, I’m happy to announce that we’ve added new apps to the AppGuru App Catalog. The newbies are Salesforce.com and Box.</p>
<p>Our strategy with AppGuru is to enable admins to manage all of the cloud apps that are critical to their businesses. We’ve been prioritizing the list based on feedback. Sync and store apps continue to be the number one request because of the security risks they can pose to a company’s data and IP. But we’re also working on integrations with other popular collaboration, helpdesk, Finance, and HR applications.</p>
<p>We believe that a delightful experience is what separates the great apps from the good apps. So, we’ve spent a great deal of time improving the way the app tiles animate when you’re interacting with them. Play around to get a feel for it. We’d love to know what you think.</p>
<p>What apps would you like to see in AppGuru? If you’re already a user, check out our feedback community which you can access from within AppGuru.  Here you can suggest or vote on apps as well as new features and functionality.</p>
<p>If you haven’t joined us on the preview yet, you can sign up <a href="https://preview.appguru.com/welcome/requestcall/form.htm">here</a>.</p>

			<div id="post-1847" class="post-1847 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-app-management category-appguru category-it-management tag-appguru-2 tag-productivity-apps tag-software tag-technology">
							<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="https://blogmeinblog.wordpress.com/2013/06/20/new-innovation-in-the-appguru-preview-part-1-group-support/" rel="bookmark">New innovation in the AppGuru Preview, Part 1: Group&nbsp;Support</a></h2>			
			<div class="entry-meta">
									<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="https://blogmeinblog.wordpress.com/2013/06/20/new-innovation-in-the-appguru-preview-part-1-group-support/" title="11:03 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">June 20, 2013</span></a>					<span class="by-author"><span class="sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="https://blogmeinblog.wordpress.com/author/ebisceglia/" title="View all posts by Eric Bisceglia" rel="author">Eric Bisceglia</a></span> </span>							</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

				<div class="entry-content">
				<p>It’s been almost 4 weeks since we opened the doors on the AppGuru preview, and we’re happy to announce some new capabilities, and, yes, some new apps.  We’ll provide a deeper dive – and quick demos – on all the new features in the coming days, starting with one of our top requests: group or team support.  But before I get into the details, I wanted to first thank each and every one of our customers for your continued feedback and ideas. It’s always gratifying to know that we have such a reliable and insightful base of people to help us in our journey to build great products.</p>
<p>So, with that in mind, here’s a look at the new group support. We’d love to hear your thoughts:</p>
<p><a href="https://blogmeinblog.files.wordpress.com/2013/06/appguru_groups_screenshot.png"><img class="size-medium wp-image-1849 aligncenter" alt="AppGuru_groups_screenshot" src="https://blogmeinblog.files.wordpress.com/2013/06/appguru_groups_screenshot.png?w=300&#038;h=178" width="300" height="178" /></a></p>
<p>Group support, or more accurately nested group support, allows you to organize your users into whatever group structure makes sense for your business. It is also our first step toward broader Active Directory support; where we’ll enable you to replicate your AD group structure in AppGuru.</p>
<p>This gives you the ability to apply policies at the organization unit level – teams, departments, office locations, accounts, etc. – and apply these policies across appropriate apps under management.  The result is dramatically simplified on-boarding and the ability to ensure that new users are automatically assigned to the apps you’ve set up for their respective organizations or teams.</p>
<p>You can see it in action below, or even better, try it out for yourself.  It’s now live for all AppGuru users.  If you haven’t joined us on the preview yet, you can sign up <a href="https://preview.appguru.com/welcome/requestcall/form.htm">here</a>.</p>
<p>Don’t be shy. We want to hear more about what you like, loathe and anything in between.  And feel free to pile on over at our feedback community once you’ve joined the preview.</p>

							<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="https://blogmeinblog.wordpress.com/2013/05/21/introducing-appguru-its-answer-to-managing-cloud-apps-in-the-workplace/" rel="bookmark">Introducing AppGuru: IT’s Answer to Managing Cloud Apps in the&nbsp;Workplace</a></h2>			
			<div class="entry-meta">
									<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="https://blogmeinblog.wordpress.com/2013/05/21/introducing-appguru-its-answer-to-managing-cloud-apps-in-the-workplace/" title="8:03 am" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date">May 21, 2013</span></a>					<span class="by-author"><span class="sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="https://blogmeinblog.wordpress.com/author/michaelksimon/" title="View all posts by Michael Simon" rel="author">Michael Simon</a></span> </span>							</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

				<div class="entry-content">
				<p>Today, we’re introducing the <a href="http://preview.appguru.com" target="_blank">preview of a new offering</a> built to help IT professionals bolster their strategic value in the cloud era.  The latest addition to our IT management portfolio, AppGuru provides IT pros with the tools needed to embrace, secure and manage the rapid influx of business and employee-introduced cloud applications into the workplace – the types of apps that often gain a foothold with little or no IT involvement.  While control is certainly part of this, the real story – the real value – is how a solution like AppGuru can help IT adopt new management approaches to encourage the broader, secure use of employee-introduced solutions.</p>
<p>In a nutshell, AppGuru brings together Identity-as-a-Service (IDaaS) features, provisioning and policy management for cloud apps, ranging from SaaS enterprise applications to cloud productivity suites to employee-introduced business and personal apps.  It’s a product that we believe can help IT be better positioned to be an expert advisor to business sponsors and a partner with employees in the cloud app era. And it’s a product whose genesis ties back to the intersection of LogMeIn’s IT management and cloud app businesses.</p>
<p>Our own cloud apps – LogMeIn Free/Pro, <a href="http://join.me">join.me</a>, and most recently <a href="https://cubby.com">Cubby</a> &#8212; have attracted millions of users, and they, like most popular cloud apps, have become widely popular in the workplace, often outside of IT’s purview.  After some internal grassroots adoption, these apps begin to bubble up to IT decision makers, who might recognize the benefit of such tools, and then look to us to help them with broader team or company-wide adoption, as well as better management.</p>
<p>At the same time, we’re hearing a consistent message from our large base of IT customers: They are not looking to play the role of ‘gatekeeper’ when it comes to app usage but are struggling to find a way to best manage users, subscriptions, policies, and of course, information sharing. Many of them are even seeing cloud apps – like other kinds of bring-your-own tech – not as a threat to IT-procured applications, but rather as an opportunity to save money on underutilized apps while deferring to employees’ preferences.</p>
<p>We see AppGuru as a product born from that unique perspective. That said, we’d rather have IT pros tell us whether or not we’ve hit the mark, and help us to help them with new updates and capabilities to meet their evolving needs.</p>
<p>So whether you’re an IT pro, a business user using our cloud apps, or an existing customer of our IT management offerings, take a look, and let us know what you think.</p>
