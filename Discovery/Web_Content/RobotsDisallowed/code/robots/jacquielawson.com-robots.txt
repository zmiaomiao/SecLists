			<h2>Welcome to jacquielawson.com!</h2>
			<h1><a href="http://www.jacquielawson.com/cards">The classiest e-cards on the web</a></h1>
<p>Back in the year 2000, Jacquie Lawson, an English artist living in the picturesque village of Lurgashall in Southern England, created an animated Christmas card featuring her dog and cats, and her 15th-century cottage, and sent it to a few friends for their amusement.  Those friends sent the e-card to others, and within weeks Jacquie was inundated with requests from all over the world to design more e-cards. In February 2002 she teamed up with a few friends and family members to create jacquielawson.com.</p>
<p>Over the years the collection has grown to a current total of 332 e-cards, each one showing a degree of artistry and attention to detail rarely seen in the medium of e-cards. Even the music is composed and arranged specially for each card!  We now have a range of <a href="http://www.jacquielawson.com/cards/birthday">birthday e-cards</a>, <a href="http://www.jacquielawson.com/cards/christmas">Christmas e-cards</a>, <a href="http://www.jacquielawson.com/cards/thankyou">thank you e-cards</a>, and so on, featuring <a href="http://www.jacquielawson.com/cards/dog">dogs</a>, <a href="http://www.jacquielawson.com/cards/cat">cats</a>, <a href="http://www.jacquielawson.com/cards/teddybear">teddy bears</a>, <a href="http://www.jacquielawson.com/cards/floral">flowers</a>, and many other subjects.</p>
<p>A modest yearly <a href="http://www.jacquielawson.com/membership.asp">membership fee</a> gives you access to the entire range of e-cards and allows you to send as many greetings as you like.  You can even get a further discount by <a href="http://www.jacquielawson.com/join1.asp">joining</a> for two years!</p>
<p>We hope you enjoy browsing our e-cards, and if you're not already a member, we hope you will <a href="http://www.jacquielawson.com/join1.asp">consider joining us</a> and sharing Jacquie's delightful animations with your friends and family.</p>
			<h3><a href="http://www.jacquielawson.com/news">Behind the Scenes</a></h3>
<p>Each card we make takes anything from a few weeks to several months to complete.  Many of the elements of the animation are actually painted by hand (using real paint, brushes and paper!) because the textures and colours achieved that way are so much more attractive than the electronic equivalent.  These paintings are then scanned into electronic form, and the laborious process of animation starts.  Finally, the music is created to accent or complement the animation.</p>
<p>Every so often we'll be giving you a more detailed insight into what the team has been up to.  <a href="http://www.jacquielawson.com/news">Click here</a> to see the latest news!</p>
